About

Hi, I'm Akshat Shrinate, an engineer from Gorakhpur, India, driven by a lifelong fascination with how machines sense, think, and interact with the world. I enjoy bridging the gap between hardware and AI, combining mechanics, electronics, and learning algorithms to create autonomous machines.

I hold a Bachelor in Technology in Electronics and Communication Engineering from Punjab Engineering College, Chandigarh, with a minor specialization in Digital VLSI Technologies. My journey has taken me from analyzing cutting-edge engineering patents at Copperpod IP to my current role as Research Staff at the Bio-Inspired Robotic Systems and Design (BIRD) Laboratory, IIT Jodhpur. There, I develop controllers, write embedded software, and integrate hardware with intelligent algorithms for mobile robots and quadrupeds.

What's Next?

My interests lie at the intersection of reinforcement learning, embedded AI, control theory, and legged locomotion. I am currently exploring Master's opportunities to deepen my expertise in these areas. My long-term goal is to advance bipedal locomotion by combining rigorous classical control with modern learning-based approaches, enabling agile, adaptive machines capable of operating reliably in complex real-world environments.
